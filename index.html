High Tide in Lowtown

Kurt Sullivan (Sully)

That would be me. Relax I’m not some pompous fame seeker looking to be piped into your life all hi-rez. This is just a record, and I thought you should at least know the source telling you how it all went down. I’m no probability seer or nothing, but I’ve learned over the years to trust my gut. It’s been right more often than not and right now it’s telling me something big is about to go down. I mean big like future generations will talk about these times. If this city even has future generations. Before I come off like a doomsday evangelist let me just set the stage, and you can decide if this is a mad man’s ramblings or the foreword to a cities last will and testament.

Aranea

To most city state of Aranea might as well be the whole verse. From the suits staring down on their corporate fiefdoms from their neon laden ivory towers, to tweekers in the piss scented allies watching the pretty colors dance on the back of their eyelids. Every one of us spends their life in the cage of concrete and wires. The truth is this cage is the only thing keeping us from getting cut to ribbons by the science projects gone feral outside the walls. Sure you can get bopped and rocked by the folk’s city side, but hey better the monster you know.

Central Pillar Department

The Central Pillar Police Department is the closest thing the common person can get to arbiters of justice. There was a time when I was much more naïve that I thought they meted out this justice in a mostly impartial way. Who would have thought a private police force would turn a blind eye when someone who their employers deem untouchable decides they are above the law? Well a few years on the force opened my eyes on that matter. Just not in time to keep myself from hoping out of the frying pan and straight onto the heating element. Still they do their best to keep a semblance of peace in the city proper. Just don’t make the mistake of thinking they will take your side over those that fund them.

Roh Zi

For someone that’s been exposed to the ragged depths of west district; Roh Zi still manages to wear a smile brighter than the neon signs littering the street outside her bar. First met the kid working the case that cost me my badge. Her old man called her in M.I.A. a few times and I noticed the higher ups didn’t seem too interested in tracking her down. Only took a day, and a few squabbles with some local info bros for me to track down the traffickers that snatched her for their meat puppet operation. Managed to get her out, but had to bag and tag a few bullet-stoppers. In the process one of the clients managed to get greased. Turns out he was a somebody, and my career got swept under the rug with the rest of the dirt during the cover up. Hey at least Roh Zi cuts me a break renting office space above The Jive Dive.

The Jive Dive

After ending things with the department on a low note, I had to find a way to scrape by. I ended up using the only skills I had to set myself up as a P.I. Not the most luxurious jig in town, but at least I can work on my own terms now, and make enough to keep my kibble card flush with creds. Rented some space above The Jive bar on the low after Roh Zi took it over for her pops. It’s got what passes for class in this part of town, although the folks in Central district would laugh at what passes for fancy around these parts. The live music draws a good crowd, and the fact that most of my clients could use a drink while telling me their woes means having a bar downstairs is handy.

Synth Junkie

Sure the common perception about these guys is just to see them as addicts, but in honesty they are more of a cult at this point. The tenets of these advocates of augments are easy enough to boil down. Life sucks for humans; so replace as much of yourself with tech until you're barely human. Cut yourself down to the lowest sum of organic matter you can, throw it in the fanciest chassis you can scrape together, and pop in a B.T.L. chip for good measure. Do this and you too can be the happiest tin man on your block. That’s their claim at least...

Live Arena

The modern day gladiators licensed to fight challengers nearly any place or time; sponsored by corporations and scrap shops trying to prove themselves. Synth junkies, street boppers, criminals with their debt leased out, or just fame seekers; sponsors don’t care about your motivations. If you make their tech look good while literally beating their competitions champ with its own arm you just ripped off you’ve got what it takes. The fights make for a great distraction to the fans, but just be sure to not be within a city block when one breaks out. Sure they are insured but you won’t survive to spend the credits if you get caught in the crossfire.

Gang Syndicates

The corporations divide up the city. Wage acts of war against each other, and make backdoor deals to end the conflicts the next day. In this back and forth some things slip through the cracks, and the syndicates are there to pick them up. Anything not locked down that they can take control of that isn’t important enough to “police” is up for grabs and the syndicates use their numbers and network to make it theirs. There are rumors that the most bold of them will even sell their services to one corporation to harass another.

Expansionist Riots

Walk the streets long enough and you’ll see the calling cards of the Expansionist in the form of graffiti tags, arson debris, and hacked holo ad projectors. Simple fact is the city is growing; that is to say the population is growing. The area we are confined to however isn’t. So the lines are drawn. Playing the role of the parent you have the ruling Corps. Saying the risk of contamination is to dangerous to expand into the wilds, and playing the role of the tantrum throwing child you have the Expansionist claiming if we don’t expand we will all die from the stagnation. I can’t really make a call on who is right, but like most I’m getting tired of the argument that seems to only be getting more heated.
